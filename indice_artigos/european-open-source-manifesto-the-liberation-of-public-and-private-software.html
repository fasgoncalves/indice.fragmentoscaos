European Open-Source Manifesto

What we propose is not a closed project nor a portal that dies after two reports. It is an ecosystem: funding, standards, field teams, certification, intelligent public procurement and a community that does not abandon anyone mid-journey.

  6. European training and certification programmes.
  7. Mandatory upstream contribution for publicly funded software.
  8. ‚ÄúSME First‚Äù programme with remote migration support.

Benefits Overview: Proprietary vs Open-Source

Below is a pragmatic, decision-oriented summary. It does not claim one model is universally ‚Äúbetter‚Äù in every scenario: it highlights where each model typically excels ‚Äî and where its hidden costs tend to appear.

Proprietary Model ‚Äî Typical Benefits
  • Single-vendor accountability: clear contractual responsibility and escalation paths.
  • Integrated ‚Äúsuite‚Äù experience: tight coupling across tools (sometimes reducing integration effort).
  • Perceived simplicity for procurement: standardised licensing and vendor-led roadmaps.
  • Vendor-provided compliance artifacts: documentation, attestations, and enterprise support bundles.
  • Short-term onboarding speed: quick adoption when organisations are already aligned to the vendor ecosystem.
Common trade-off: benefits often come with long-term lock-in costs and reduced negotiation power.
Open-Source Model ‚Äî Typical Benefits
  • Digital sovereignty: portability, independence, and freedom to change providers.
  • Auditability & transparency: code review, independent audits, and verifiable fixes.
  • Cost structure control: spend shifts from ‚Äúrent‚Äù to support, engineering, and training.
  • Interoperability-friendly: strong alignment with open standards and ecosystem integration.
  • Innovation leverage: roadmap can be influenced by contributions and public funding priorities.
  • Local economic impact: budgets support local integrators, SMEs and skilled jobs.
Common requirement: governance, skills, and support contracts must be planned ‚Äî freedom needs operational discipline.
